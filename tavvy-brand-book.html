Brand Story
Why Tavvy exists and what we're here to change.
We believe every experience deserves more than a number. Real people sharing real signals — that's how trust is built.

The Problem

Star ratings have become meaningless. They're gamed by bots, inflated by incentives, and tell you nothing about what actually matters. Is this coffee shop good for working? Is this contractor on time? Does this restaurant have outdoor seating? A "4.5 stars" can't answer any of that.

The Solution

Tavvy replaces generic star ratings with specific, verified signals from real people. Instead of a number, you get "Cozy Vibes," "Best Flat White," "Punctual," "Fairly Priced" — the information you actually need to make a confident decision.

Logo Usage Rules
Protect the logo's integrity. Consistency builds recognition.

Clear Space

Always maintain minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the dot in the icon. No other elements should enter this zone.

CLEAR SPACE

Minimum Size

Horizontal Logo

Minimum width: 80px

Stacked Logo

Minimum width: 60px

Do
  • Use on approved backgrounds only (white, dark, purple)
  • Maintain clear space at all times
  • Use horizontal when width allows
  • Use stacked for square placements
  • Use icon-only below 100px width
  • Use provided PNG files at 300ppi
Don't
  • Place on busy photos without a solid container
  • Rotate, skew, or distort the logo
  • Change the logo colors
  • Add shadows, outlines, or glow effects
  • Place on low-contrast backgrounds
  • Stretch or compress the proportions
  • Recreate or redraw any part of the logo
  • Lock up with other logos without approval

Type Rules

Do
  • Use sentence case for headlines
  • Keep "tavvy" lowercase in logo contexts
  • Use proper case "Tavvy" in running text
  • Maintain generous line height (1.5-1.7)
Don't
  • Use all-caps for more than 3 words
  • Use decorative or script fonts
  • Set body text smaller than 14px
  • Use thin/light weights for key messages

Photography
Authentic, diverse, and warm. Our imagery reflects real people having real experiences.
Do
  • Use authentic, natural photography
  • Show real people in real places
  • Capture diversity in subjects — ages, backgrounds, professions
  • Use warm, natural lighting
  • Show candid moments and genuine expressions
  • Show the app in context (phone in hand, real usage)
  • Capture the environment — show what makes a place unique
Don't
  • Use generic stock with forced smiles
  • Use overly filtered or heavily processed images
  • Show star ratings or competitor UI positively
  • Use dark, moody, or overly dramatic photography
  • Use images that feel staged or corporate
  • Show empty or pristine environments (we're about real life)
  • Use imagery that skews to only one demographic

Photo Treatment

When using photos in marketing materials:

Purple Overlay

For hero images: apply a purple (#8A05BE) overlay at 60-70% opacity. White text on top.

Dark Overlay

For backgrounds: apply dark (#17013A) overlay at 70-80% opacity. Maintains readability.

No Overlay

For editorial/blog: use photos at full brightness with no overlay. Text below, not on top.

Campaign:
Stars Are Dead
Our launch campaign positions Tavvy as the modern replacement for broken star ratings.
#StarsAreDead

Visual Language

★★★★☆

The Old Way

Muted golds, grays, faded. Represents the past. Always appears lifeless and static.

Cozy Vibes Punctual

The Tavvy Way

Vivid purple and teal. Represents the future. Always appears alive and dynamic.

Campaign Phases

Phase 1: Ignite (Weeks 1-2)

"Stars Are Dead" provocation. Make people question star ratings. TikTok, Reels, Shorts.

Phase 2: Educate (Weeks 3-4)

"I'm a Traditional Review" campaign. Show how Tavvy works. YouTube, Meta Ads.

Phase 3: Acquire Pros (Weeks 5-6)

Target service professionals. eCard showcase. LinkedIn, Facebook.

Phase 4: Sustain (Ongoing)

15s snackable content. UGC. A/B testing. Organic social.

Ad End Screen Standard

Every video ad must end with the Tavvy stacked logo centered on either the dark (#17013A) or purple (#8A05BE) background. Include the campaign tagline below the logo.
