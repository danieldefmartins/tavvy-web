## 1. Brand Overview

### Who We Are
Tavvy is a trust-based review and discovery platform that replaces meaningless star ratings with real, specific signals from real people. We help consumers make confident decisions and help professionals build verified reputations.

### Brand Personality
- **Confident** -- We know we're better than star ratings. We say it clearly.
- **Approachable** -- We're the trusted friend who gives you real advice.
- **Modern** -- We look and feel like the future, not another legacy review site.
- **Bold** -- We're the new player taking on giants. Our brand should feel fearless.

### Brand Positioning
> "Real experiences, not fake stars."

Tavvy is the anti-star-rating. While competitors reduce rich human experiences to a single number, Tavvy captures what actually matters -- specific, verified signals that tell you exactly what to expect.

## 2. Logo

### The Icon
The Tavvy icon consists of two overlapping pill shapes forming a checkmark/heart hybrid with a centered dot. It represents:
- **Checkmark** = Trust and verification
- **Heart** = People and connection
- **Two overlapping elements** = Meeting, discovery, bringing people together
- **The dot** = A meeting point, a moment of connection

### Logo Usage Rules

**DO:**
- Use the logo on approved background colors only (white, dark #17013A, purple #8A05BE)
- Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the dot around the logo
- Use the horizontal version when width allows, stacked when space is square
- Use the icon-only version at sizes below 100px wide

**DON'T:**
- Place the full-color logo on busy photos without a solid background container
- Rotate, skew, distort, or add effects to the logo
- Change the logo colors
- Add drop shadows, outlines, or glows
- Place on backgrounds that reduce contrast (medium grays, similar purples)
- Recreate or redraw any part of the logo

### Color Usage Rules

- **Tavvy Purple** is the dominant brand color. It should be the most visible color across all touchpoints.
- **Tavvy Teal** is the accent. Use it to draw attention to interactive elements, Signal tags, and key information.
- Never use purple and teal at equal weight -- purple leads, teal accents.
- On dark backgrounds, use white text. On light backgrounds, use Tavvy Dark text.
- Never place Tavvy Teal text on white backgrounds (insufficient contrast for accessibility).

## 4. Typography

### Primary Typeface
The Tavvy wordmark uses a custom rounded sans-serif typeface. For all other brand communications:

**Headlines:** Use a rounded, bold sans-serif (e.g., Nunito Bold, Poppins Bold, or similar)
**Body text:** Use a clean sans-serif (e.g., Inter, DM Sans, or similar)
**Code/Technical:** Use a monospace font (e.g., JetBrains Mono, Fira Code)

### Type Hierarchy

| Level | Weight | Size (web) | Usage |
|-------|--------|-----------|-------|
| H1 | Bold | 48-64px | Hero headlines, campaign titles |
| H2 | Bold | 32-40px | Section headers |
| H3 | Semi-Bold | 24-28px | Card titles, sub-sections |
| Body | Regular | 16-18px | Paragraphs, descriptions |
| Caption | Regular | 12-14px | Labels, metadata, timestamps |

### Type Rules
- Always use sentence case for headlines ("Stars are dead" not "Stars Are Dead")
- The brand name is always lowercase: **tavvy** (never "Tavvy" in the logo, though proper case is acceptable in running text)
- Never use all-caps for more than 3 words at a time

## 6. Signal Tags Style

Signal tags are core to the Tavvy product. They should always appear as:

- **Shape:** Rounded pill / capsule (matching the logo's pill shapes)
- **Background:** Tavvy Teal (#00C2CB) at 15% opacity, or Lavender Light (#F4F0FF)
- **Text:** Tavvy Dark (#17013A), semi-bold
- **Border:** 1px Tavvy Teal (#00C2CB) at 30% opacity (optional)
- **Examples:** `Cozy Vibes` `Best Flat White` `Punctual` `Fairly Priced`

Signal tags should feel lightweight, colorful, and easy to scan -- the visual opposite of a heavy, singular star rating.

## 7. Photography & Imagery Style

### Do
- Use authentic, natural photography -- real people, real places
- Show diversity in subjects -- different ages, backgrounds, professions
- Warm lighting, candid moments, genuine expressions
- Show the app in context (phone in hand, real-world usage)

### Don't
- Use generic stock photography with forced smiles
- Use overly filtered or heavily edited images
- Show star ratings or competitor UI in positive contexts
- Use dark, moody, or overly dramatic photography (we're approachable, not intimidating)

## 8. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
- **Confident, not arrogant:** "Stars are dead" not "Other platforms are terrible"
- **Clear, not dumbed down:** Explain simply without being patronizing
- **Witty, not snarky:** Humor that makes people smile, not cringe
- **Direct, not aggressive:** State facts, let the audience draw conclusions

### Writing Examples

| Instead of... | Write... |
|--------------|---------|
| "We're the best review platform ever" | "Real experiences, not fake stars" |
| "Yelp and Google are terrible" | "Stars tell you nothing. Signals tell you everything." |
| "Sign up now!!!" | "Join the future of reviews" |
| "Our proprietary AI-powered algorithm" | "Real people sharing what actually matters" |

## 9. Campaign: "Stars Are Dead"

### Campaign Taglines
- Primary: **"Stars Are Dead"**
- Secondary: **"Real Experiences, Not Fake Stars"**
- CTA: **"Stop Guessing. Start Experiencing."**
- Hashtag: **#StarsAreDead**

### Campaign Visual Rules
- Star ratings should always appear in **muted gold/gray** -- the past, fading
- Tavvy Signals should appear in **vivid purple/teal** -- the future, alive
- Every ad should end with the Tavvy logo on dark (#17013A) or purple (#8A05BE) background
