1. What is Tavvy?

The review platform that actually helps your business

Tavvy is a new kind of review platform that replaces meaningless star ratings with specific signals from real customers. Instead of a "4.2 stars" that tells nobody anything, your restaurant gets signals like:

🍝 Fresh Pasta ×575 😊 Friendly Staff ×420 🇮🇹 Authentic Italian ×602 🕯️ Cozy & Intimate ×389 🏛️ Old School ×540 💵 Cash Only ×667 ⏰ Long Wait ×350

These signals tell potential customers exactly what to expect — what's great, what the vibe is like, and what to watch out for. It's honest, specific, and actually useful.

Why restaurants love Tavvy: No commission fees. No pay-per-lead. No fake review manipulation. Just real signals from real customers that help you build a genuine reputation.

2. How Signals Work

Three categories tell your customers everything

✅

The Good

What your restaurant does best. Fresh Pasta, Great Wine List, Friendly Staff, Fast Service — the specific things that make you special.

💜

The Vibe

What it feels like to be there. Cozy & Intimate, Date Night Perfect, Old School, Bustling — the atmosphere customers can expect.

⚠️

Heads Up

Honest practical info. Cash Only, Reservation Required, Long Wait — things customers need to know before visiting. This builds trust.

How customers leave signals

Customers don't write long reviews. They simply tap signals that match their experience. Each tap (1-3 intensity) contributes to your signal profile. The more people agree on a signal, the stronger it becomes.

The Living Score

Signals fade over 6 months. This means your profile always reflects your current quality — not something someone said 3 years ago. If you improve your service, your signals update automatically.

5. Creating Your Digital Menu

A beautiful menu that makes customers hungry

Tavvy's digital menu is free, beautiful, and designed to make your food look irresistible. No monthly fees, no commissions.

How to add your menu

Go to tavvy.com/place/[your-id]/menu-editor to access the menu editor. From there you can:

Create categories

Organize by: Appetizers, Salads, Pasta, Main Dishes, Desserts, Drinks — whatever fits your menu. Set meal periods (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner) for each category.

Add dishes

For each dish: name, description, price, and a photo. Upload your own photos or paste image URLs. The better the photo, the more orders you'll get.

Tag dietary info

Mark items as: Vegan 🌱, Vegetarian, Gluten-Free 🌾, Dairy-Free, Nut-Free. Set spicy levels: 🌶️ Mild, 🌶️🌶️ Spicy, 🌶️🌶️🌶️ Hot. Optionally add calorie counts.

Mark specials

Toggle "Popular" 🔥 for your best sellers. Toggle "New" ✨ for recent additions. These badges appear on the menu and attract attention.

Two menu views

Your menu automatically appears in two beautiful formats:

📋

Classic View

Magazine-style layout. All items visible with photos, descriptions, and prices. Great for browsing the full menu quickly.

📱

Gallery View

Full-screen food photos. Customers swipe between dishes like Instagram Stories. Each dish fills the entire screen. Stunning.

Allergen Filters

Customers can filter your menu by dietary needs. When they tap "Gluten-Free", non-matching items fade out. This is legally important in many states and shows you care about all customers.

6. Gallery Menu View

Full-screen food photography that sells

The gallery view is your most powerful selling tool. Each dish gets a full-screen, 9:16 portrait card — the same format as Instagram Reels and TikTok.

Tips for great gallery photos

  • Shoot in portrait mode (9:16 ratio). Landscape photos will be cropped.
  • Natural lighting makes food look best. Avoid harsh flash.
  • Close-up shots with the dish filling the frame work best.
  • Show the texture — steam rising, cheese pulling, sauce dripping.
  • Clean backgrounds — remove clutter around the plate.

Customers can also upload their own food photos. When they do, you can link those real customer photos to specific menu items — showing what the food actually looks like, not just your marketing photos.

8. QR Codes for Tables

From scan to order in seconds

Generate free QR codes for each table. When customers scan, they see your beautiful menu instantly — no app download needed.

How to set up

Go to your QR page

Visit tavvy.com/place/[your-id]/qr to access the QR generator.

Download or print

Click "Download PNG" to save the QR code, or click "Print" to print directly. The QR is designed to look clean on table tents.

Place on tables

Print QR codes on table tents, stickers, or placards. Customers scan with their phone camera — opens the menu instantly in their browser.

Pro tip: Customers don't need to download any app. The QR opens your full-screen menu directly in their phone's browser. Works on every phone.

9. In-Restaurant Ordering

Customers order from their phone, you confirm on your tablet

With Tavvy Pro ($29/month), customers can order directly from their table — no waiter needed to take the order.

How it works for customers

Scan the table QR

Customer scans the QR code at their table. The menu opens with ordering enabled.

Build their order

They browse the menu, tap "+" to add items, adjust quantities, and add special notes ("no onions", "extra spicy").

QR verification

Before submitting, they scan the table QR again. This proves they're physically at the table — no fake orders.

Real-time status

After ordering, their phone shows live updates: "Confirmed" → "Preparing" → "Ready" → "Served".

10. Managing Orders

Your command center for incoming orders

The order dashboard is designed for restaurant speed. Open it on a tablet and keep it running all day.

Access it at: tavvy.com/place/[your-id]/dashboard

The Kanban board

Orders flow through four columns:

New Orders

Fresh orders just placed. Tap "Confirm" to accept or "Cancel" with a reason.

Confirmed

Accepted orders. Kitchen ticket auto-prints. Tap "Start Preparing" when the kitchen begins.

Preparing

Being cooked. Tap "Mark Ready" when the food is done.

Ready

Waiting for pickup/delivery to table. Tap "Mark Served" to complete.

Key features

  • Audio notification — a ding sounds when new orders arrive
  • Urgent alerts — orders waiting 5+ minutes pulse red
  • Auto-print — kitchen tickets print automatically when you confirm an order
  • Real-time — no refreshing needed, orders appear instantly
  • Daily stats — see total orders and revenue at the top

Hardware needed: Just a tablet (iPad recommended) connected to a thermal receipt printer via WiFi or USB. That's it. No special POS hardware required.

11. Understanding Your Analytics

See what customers love and what to improve

Signal analytics

Your signal profile tells you exactly what customers think:

  • Top signals — Your strongest qualities. Use these in your marketing.
  • Growing signals — Qualities gaining traction. Double down on what's working.
  • Heads Up signals — Common complaints. Fix these to improve your score.

Menu analytics

  • Menu views — How many people opened your menu
  • Menu shares — How many times your menu was shared
  • Per-dish views — Which dishes get the most attention in gallery mode
  • Per-dish shares — Which dishes people share with friends
  • Auto-popular badges — Tavvy automatically marks dishes as "Popular" based on real signal data

What to do with this data

  • If a dish gets lots of views but few orders → improve the description or photo
  • If a dish gets shared a lot → it's your hero item. Feature it as Chef's Pick.
  • If "Slow Service" signal is growing → address kitchen or staffing issues
  • If "Friendly Staff" is your top signal → mention it in your marketing

14. Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

Do customers need to download an app?

No. Everything works in the browser. Customers scan the QR code and your menu opens instantly — no app download, no signup required to browse.

Can I update my menu from my phone?

Yes. The menu editor works on any device. Change prices, mark items as sold out, or add new dishes from your phone in seconds.

What if I get fake or unfair signals?

Signals fade over 6 months (Living Score). One bad signal won't hurt you if 100 others are positive. The system is designed to reflect consensus, not individual complaints.

Can I respond to signals?

Coming soon. We're building a feature for owners to respond to signals publicly — showing customers you listen and care.

What printer do I need for kitchen tickets?

Any printer connected to your tablet works. The system uses standard browser printing. For best results, use a thermal receipt printer (Epson or Star) connected via WiFi.

Can I disable ordering and just use the menu?

Absolutely. Ordering is a Pro feature. On the Free plan, customers can browse your beautiful menu but ordering is disabled. You can upgrade anytime.

How do I get more signals?

Put QR codes on your tables. When customers scan to see the menu, they're one tap away from leaving signals. The easier you make it, the more signals you'll get.

Can I use Tavvy with my existing POS?

Tavvy works independently of your POS. Orders come through the Tavvy dashboard, and kitchen tickets print separately. We're working on POS integrations for 2027.
